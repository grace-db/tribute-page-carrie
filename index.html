Carrie Fisher

career

  • 1970s
    • 1957 - Fisher made her film debut in the comedy Shampoo at the age of 17
    • 1977 - Fisher starred as Princess Leia in Star Wars (later retitled Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope), which became her most well-known role
    • 1978 - Made an appearance as the love interest in the TV special Ringo, and she also was in the ABC-TV film Leave Yesterday Behind, as well as a couple of other roles
  • 1980s
    • 1980 - She appeared on Broadway in Censored Scenes from King Kong. That same year, she reprised her role as Princess Leia in The Empire Strikes Back
      • July 12, 1980 - Fisher appeared with Star Wars co-stars on the cover of Rolling Stone to promote the film
    • 1983 - Fisher returned to the role of Princess Leia in Return of the Jedi
    • 1986 - She starred in the film Hannah and Her Sisters
    • 1987 - Fisher published her first novel, Postcards from the Edge; a semi-autobiographical in the sense that she fictionalized and satirized real-life events such as her drug addiction of the late 1970s
    • 1987 - She was in the Australian film The Time Guardian
    • 1989 - Fisher played a major supporting role in the the film, When Harry Met Sally..., as well as appearing with Tom Hanks in The 'Burbs
  • 1990s
    • 1990 - Columbia Pictures released a film version of Postcards from the Edge, casting Fisher and several others; that year, Fisher also published her novel Surrender the Pink
    • 1991 - Fisher appeared in the fantasy comedy film Drop Dead Fred, and played a therapist in Austin Powers: International Man of Mystery
    • 1993 - She published another book, Delusions of Grandma

      • In the 90s, Fisher also wrote for several films and television scripts--often going uncredited.

  • 2000s
    • 2000 - Fisher was in the film Scream 3
    • 2001 - Fisher acted in the comedy, Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back, and she co-wrote the TV comedy film, These Old Broads, of which she was also co-executive producer
    • 2003 - Fisher played Mother Superior in Charlie's Angels: Full Throttle

Fisher was one of the top script doctors in Hollywood, and she played plenty more roles throughout her lifetime, as well as reprising her early role of Leia Skywalker in the Sequel Trilogy of the Star Wars Saga.

advocacy

Carrie Fisher was very passionate about advocating for several causes, including women's advocacy, animal rights, and LGBT causes.

In 2016, Fisher was awarded the Annual Outstanding Lifetime Achievement Award in Cultural Humanism from Harvard College.

She was an activist to help spread awareness of addiction, mental illness, and agnosticism. She brought light on these issues with creativity and empathy.

personal life

Fisher struggled with drug abuse, which led her to getting the diagnosis of her having bipolar disorder.

She publicly discussed her diagnosis, revealing that she went through therapy to help with the symptoms.

Fisher had a few different romances in her life, one of which led to the birth of her only child—her daughter—Billie.

legacy

Fisher passed suddenly at age 60, reported as not breathing on a flight nearing Los Angeles. She was hospitalized, passing on the morning of December 27th, 2016 (four days later).

As heartbreaking as it was to lose such a free-spirited and beautiful soul, may we look back on the life of Carrie Fisher and see a legacy full of color. She inspired so many people through her acting, writing, and her life in general. Fisher brought love with her, even in moments of struggle. And now, she is forever among the stars.
